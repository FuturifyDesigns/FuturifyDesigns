Featured Aquatic Creatures

Clownfish

Clownfish, also known as anemonefish, are small, brightly colored fish found in warm waters of the Indian and Pacific Oceans, including the Great Barrier Reef and the Red Sea.

They have a symbiotic relationship with sea anemones, where the anemone provides protection and the clownfish provides nutrients through its waste and helps lure prey.

Quick Facts

  • Scientific Name: Amphiprioninae
  • Size: 10-18 cm (4-7 inches)
  • Lifespan: 6-10 years in the wild
  • Diet: Omnivorous (algae, plankton, small crustaceans)
  • All clownfish are born male and can change sex to become the dominant female

Seahorse

Seahorses are small marine fish in the genus Hippocampus. They are named for their equine appearance, with bent necks and long snouted heads.

Unlike most other fish, seahorses are monogamous and mate for life. They are also unique in that the male carries the developing embryos in a brood pouch.

Quick Facts

  • Scientific Name: Hippocampus
  • Size: 1.5-35 cm (0.6-14 inches)
  • Lifespan: 1-5 years depending on species
  • Diet: Small crustaceans like mysid shrimp
  • Seahorses can change color to blend in with their surroundings

Octopus

Octopuses are soft-bodied, eight-limbed molluscs of the order Octopoda. They are among the most intelligent and behaviorally diverse of all invertebrates.

Octopuses inhabit various regions of the ocean, including coral reefs, pelagic waters, and the ocean floor. They have excellent eyesight and can change both color and texture to camouflage.

Quick Facts

  • Scientific Name: Octopoda
  • Size: 5 cm to 5 m (2 inches to 16 feet) depending on species
  • Lifespan: 6 months to 5 years depending on species
  • Diet: Crabs, shrimp, lobsters, fish, and other mollusks
  • Octopuses have three hearts and blue blood

Sea Turtle

Sea turtles are large, air-breathing reptiles that inhabit tropical and subtropical seas throughout the world. Their shells consist of a carapace and plastron.

Sea turtles have existed since the time of the dinosaurs, about 110 million years. All seven species found today are under threat from human activities.

Quick Facts

  • Scientific Name: Chelonioidea
  • Size: 60-180 cm (2-6 feet) depending on species
  • Lifespan: 50-100 years
  • Diet: Varies by species (jellyfish, seaweed, crabs, sponges, etc.)
  • Sea turtles can migrate incredibly long distances between feeding and nesting areas

Jellyfish

Jellyfish are free-swimming marine animals with umbrella-shaped bells and trailing tentacles. They are found in every ocean, from the surface to the deep sea.

Jellyfish have been around for more than 500 million years, making them the oldest multi-organ animal. They are composed of 95% water and have no brain, blood, or heart.

Quick Facts

  • Scientific Name: Scyphozoa (true jellyfish)
  • Size: 2 cm to 2 m (0.8 inches to 6.5 feet) depending on species
  • Lifespan: Few hours to several years depending on species
  • Diet: Small fish, plankton, crustaceans, and other jellyfish
  • Some jellyfish can glow in the dark (bioluminescence)

Whale Shark

The whale shark is the largest known extant fish species. Despite its size, it feeds mainly on plankton and is docile towards humans.

Whale sharks are found in open waters of tropical oceans and are rarely found in water below 21°C (70°F). They are characterized by a pattern of white spots and stripes on their dark gray back.

Quick Facts

  • Scientific Name: Rhincodon typus
  • Size: Up to 18 m (60 feet), average 12 m (40 feet)
  • Lifespan: Estimated 70-100 years
  • Diet: Plankton, krill, small fish, and squid
  • Each whale shark's spot pattern is unique, like a fingerprint

Dolphin

Dolphins are highly intelligent marine mammals known for their playful behavior and complex communication. They are part of the toothed whale family.

Dolphins are found worldwide, mostly in shallow seas of the continental shelves. They are social animals that live in pods of up to a dozen individuals.

Quick Facts

  • Scientific Name: Delphinidae (family)
  • Size: 1.7-9.5 m (5.6-31 feet) depending on species
  • Lifespan: 20-50 years depending on species
  • Diet: Fish and squid
  • Dolphins use echolocation to navigate and hunt
